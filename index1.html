Green Space and Biodiversity

By Saanj Kudve, Ruchika Chonage, Srushti Nagare

Introduction Problem Objectives Literature Survey Methodology Outcome Conclusion

Introduction

Green spaces like parks, forests, and gardens are vital for nature. Biodiversity refers to the variety of life within these spaces. Both are important for clean air, water, climate balance, and human well-being.

Problem Statement

Urbanization is reducing green spaces, leading to biodiversity loss and weaker ecosystems. This affects air purification, climate regulation, and more.

Objectives

  • Preserve and enhance green spaces
  • Protect and promote biodiversity
  • Integrate green planning in urban areas
  • Increase public awareness

Literature Survey

Studies highlight that green spaces are essential for biodiversity and ecological health. Poor urban planning reduces them, affecting both nature and humans.

Methodology

  • Area Selection: Parks, gardens, or forests
  • Data Collection: Plant/animal surveys, GIS mapping
  • Analysis: Use biodiversity indices (e.g., Shannon-Wiener)
  • Stakeholder Input: Interviews with locals/planners
  • Report: Recommendations for green space planning

Expected Outcome

The study will show that well-managed green areas lead to richer biodiversity and better living environments. It will help guide future planning and conservation.

Conclusion

Green spaces and biodiversity are key for a healthy planet. Protecting them helps nature and improves human life. Sustainable planning is essential for the future.
